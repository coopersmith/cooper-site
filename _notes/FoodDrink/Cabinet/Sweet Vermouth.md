## Recommended Brands

- **Carpano** — Carpano Antica Formula — rich, vanilla-heavy, the heavyweight choice. Stands up to bold spirits.
  Used in: [[Boulevardier]]
- **Carpano Antica** — The premium Italian sweet vermouth — vanilla, dried fruit, dark chocolate notes. Use when the cocktail can carry the weight.
  Used in: [[Double Rye Manhattan]], [[El Rey Manhattan]]
- **Cinzano 1757 Vermouth Rosso** — Restored 1757 recipe — botanically complex, herbal, more aromatic than standard Cinzano.
  Used in: [[Bitter Giuseppe]]
- **Cocchi Vermouth di Torino** — Classic Torino-style — rosa, cocoa, subtle bitter finish. Lighter than Carpano Antica.
  Used in: [[The Lucille]], [[Thou Shall Not Be Named]]
- **Dolin** — Dolin Rouge — French chambéry style. Lighter, more delicate; doesn't dominate cocktails.
  Used in: [[Bridgetown Negroni]]
- **Martini & Rossi Rosso** — Approachable, slightly sweet entry-level vermouth. Works in a pinch.
  Used in: [[Little Italy]]
- **Barbadillo Atamán Vermut** — Spanish sherry-based vermouth from the great Sanlúcar sherry house. Oxidative, nutty, drier than Italian sweet vermouths — adds savory depth in place of vanilla weight.
  Used in: [[Other Boulevardier]]
